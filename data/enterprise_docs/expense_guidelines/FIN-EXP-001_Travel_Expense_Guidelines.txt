NOVATECH ENTERPRISES
FINANCIAL POLICY — FIN-EXP-001
TRAVEL AND EXPENSE GUIDELINES
Effective Date: 1 January 2026 | Version: 3.2

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
These guidelines govern the reimbursement of business travel expenses incurred by
NovaTech employees while conducting company business. They apply to all employees
globally and to any third parties travelling on behalf of NovaTech.

2. BOOKING TRAVEL
All travel must be booked through the NovaTech Travel Portal (powered by Concur Travel).
Direct bookings with airlines or hotels are only permitted when the portal does not service
the destination. Employees must use the most cost-effective travel option that meets
reasonable business needs.

3. AIR TRAVEL
Economy class is the standard for all domestic flights and international flights under
6 hours. Business class is permitted for international flights over 6 hours with VP or
above approval. No first-class travel is permitted under any circumstance.

Maximum advance booking requirement: 14 days for domestic, 21 days for international.
Employees who book within these windows without documented emergency justification will
not be reimbursed for the premium over the economy guideline rate.

4. ACCOMMODATION
Hotels must be selected from preferred vendor list in the Travel Portal.
Maximum nightly rates (excluding taxes and fees):
  - Domestic (major cities):  USD 220 per night
  - Domestic (other cities):  USD 175 per night
  - International (developed):  USD 280 per night
  - International (emerging):  USD 200 per night

Rates above these caps require VP approval prior to booking.

5. MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT
Daily meal per diems (receipts required above USD 25 per meal):
  - Breakfast:   USD 20
  - Lunch:       USD 30
  - Dinner:      USD 60
  - Daily total: USD 110

Client entertainment expenses above USD 150 per person require VP approval and must
document: attendees (name and company), business purpose, and outcome.
Alcohol may only be claimed as part of client entertainment, not on internal trips.

6. GROUND TRANSPORT
Taxi and rideshare (Uber/Lyft) are reimbursed for business travel. Personal car use
is reimbursed at USD 0.67 per mile (2026 IRS rate). Car rental is permitted for trips
where a rental is more economical than taxis over multiple days; compact or economy
class only unless unavailable.

7. RECEIPT REQUIREMENTS
Receipts are required for all individual expenses above USD 25. Receipts must show:
vendor name, date, itemised charges, and total. Credit card statements are not
acceptable substitutes. Lost receipt affidavits may be submitted for up to 2 claims
per employee per year.

8. EXPENSE SUBMISSION
Expenses must be submitted within 30 calendar days of the expense being incurred.
Late submissions will be rejected unless supported by a documented exception approved
by Finance. Expense reports are submitted via Concur Expense.

9. PERSONAL EXPENSES NOT REIMBURSABLE
The following are not reimbursable under any circumstances: personal entertainment
(movies, personal shopping, spa), travel insurance purchased independently, seat
upgrade fees, checked bag fees beyond one bag per trip, room service on non-travel days,
minibar charges, and spouse/partner travel expenses.

10. POLICY VIOLATIONS
Fraudulent claims will result in disciplinary action including potential termination
and legal action. Finance conducts monthly audits of 10% of expense reports. All
employees certify accuracy when submitting claims.
